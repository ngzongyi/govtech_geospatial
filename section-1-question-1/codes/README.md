# HDB Resale Portal — Business Impact Analysis

Did the portal (launched Jan 2018) actually get people transacting without an
agent? 4 angles, using CEA agent records + HDB resale data, 2017–2025 (plus
history back to 1990 for one test).

## What's in the notebook

Four angles, short answer up top of each:

1. **Portal helpful?** DIY rate, lag-corrected (~2mo CEA-to-registration
   gap). Flat since 2017 (~9.5% sellers, ~38.4% buyers, 2025). One real
   bump: 2019–2020, buyer-side, likely the portal — checked against COVID,
   a bad lag fit, and agent churn first. Market volume itself: no jump either.
2. **Hurt agents' business?** Workforce, productivity, concentration, churn.
   Workforce +22% since 2017. Churn turned negative 2023–24 — one thing to watch.
3. **Market overall?** Price +53% (2017→2025) — COVID-era cycle, not the portal.
4. **Adoption by town?** DIY rate 6.3–13.4% (sellers), 31–68% (buyers) across
   towns. Smaller towns self-serve more. Maybe the biggest finding here.

Full detail, every test, every number: in the notebook.
